Going Solo: Connecting Solo Travelers

A comprehensive UX/UI case study for a travel companion finder app designed to help solo travelers connect with like-minded individuals for safer, more enriching travel experiences.

6 min read 2024 UX Research, Mobile Design

Project Overview

Going Solo is a digital platform designed to help solo travelers find travel buddies who share similar travel interests and destinations. The primary goal is to enhance the solo travel experience by providing a safe and convenient way for travelers to connect with others who might be on the same journey or looking for companionship during their travels.

The Problem

Despite the growing popularity of solo travel, many travelers still face anxiety over safety, language barriers, and loneliness. Finding a compatible travel companion is particularly challenging when friends are unavailable, or when you're new to a city and lack a social circle. There is a need for an easy way to connect with like-minded individuals for shared travel experiences, ensuring safety, companionship, and a more enriching journey.

UX Research & Competitive Analysis

I conducted extensive research on tourism categories, tourist types, and destination themes. Through competitive analysis of apps like Meetup, Tripr, Travello, and Couchsurfing, I identified key insights:

  • Most existing apps focus on group activities rather than one-on-one travel buddy matching
  • Safety and trust are common concerns among users, with many apps lacking robust verification features
  • Users appreciate features that allow them to filter potential travel companions by interests, location, and travel dates

Target Audience

Solo Travelers

Individuals who enjoy traveling alone but are open to meeting new people and sharing parts of their journey.

New Travelers

Those who are new to traveling solo and might feel more comfortable having a companion for certain parts of their trip.

Adventurous Spirits

People who love the idea of spontaneous travel and are looking for like-minded individuals to explore with.

Key Features & Solutions

Safety & Verification

Profile verification, background checks, and reliable user reviews to ensure safe connections.

Location-Based Matching

Find travelers within 100km and discover groups near you for immediate connections.

Smart Matching

Swipe-based travel buddy matching based on shared interests, destinations, and travel dates.

Community Features

Group chats, live hangouts, and country pages for building lasting travel communities.

Design Process

1
Research & Analysis

Extensive user research, competitive analysis, and market study to understand user needs and pain points.

2
User Personas & Journey Mapping

Created detailed user personas and mapped user journeys to identify key touchpoints and opportunities.

3
Wireframing & Prototyping

Developed wireframes and interactive prototypes to test user flows and gather feedback.

4
Visual Design & Testing

Created high-fidelity designs and conducted user testing to refine the experience.

Results & Impact

Working on this project allowed me to engage with a problem statement rooted in real-world business scenarios, which greatly expanded my understanding of the travel industry. Most of my findings and assumptions were informed by direct user interviews, grounding the project in real user experiences.

Throughout the project, I noticed significant improvement in my problem-solving abilities. Initially, I struggled to identify and address flaws in my designs. However, as the project progressed, I cultivated the habit of asking critical questions and thoroughly evaluating every design decision.
